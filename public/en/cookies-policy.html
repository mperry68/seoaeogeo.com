Cookies Policy

Last updated: December 4, 2025

What Are Cookies

Cookies are small text files that are placed on your computer or mobile device when you visit a website. They are widely used to make websites work more efficiently and provide information to website owners.

How We Use Cookies

Currently, Digital Relevance does not set any first-party cookies on our website. Our site is a static website that does not require cookies to function.

Third-Party Cookies

We may use third-party services that set cookies:

  • Google Maps: Our contact page includes an embedded Google Maps iframe. Google may set cookies to provide map functionality and improve user experience. These cookies are subject to Google's Privacy Policy. You can view Google's cookie information at Google's Privacy Policy.

Future Use

If we implement analytics, preference settings, or other features that require cookies in the future, we will update this policy accordingly and provide appropriate cookie consent mechanisms where required by law.

Cookie Security

While we do not currently set first-party cookies, if we implement cookies in the future, we will implement the following security measures:

  • Secure Cookies: We will use secure cookies (HTTPS-only) to ensure that cookie data is transmitted over encrypted connections.
  • HttpOnly Flag: We will use HttpOnly cookies where applicable to prevent client-side scripts from accessing cookie data, reducing the risk of cross-site scripting (XSS) attacks.
  • SameSite Attribute: We will implement SameSite cookie attributes to help protect against cross-site request forgery (CSRF) attacks.
  • Cookie Expiration: Cookies will be set with appropriate expiration dates, and we will regularly review and remove unnecessary or expired cookies.
  • No Sensitive Data: We will never store sensitive personal information, such as passwords or credit card numbers, in cookies.

Managing Cookies

Since we do not set first-party cookies, blocking cookies will not affect the core functionality of our website. However, third-party cookies from Google Maps may be blocked by your browser settings.

You can control and manage cookies in your browser settings. Most browsers allow you to:

  • View and delete cookies
  • Block all cookies or only third-party cookies
  • Set preferences for specific websites
  • Clear cookies when you close your browser

The help section of your browser will provide specific instructions on how to manage cookies. You can also use browser extensions or privacy tools to manage cookie preferences.

Changes to This Policy

We may update this Cookies Policy from time to time. We will notify you of any changes by posting the new Cookies Policy on this page and updating the "Last updated" date.
