What is Technical SEO?

Technical SEO is the process of optimizing a website's backend and infrastructure so search engines can efficiently crawl, index, and rank your content. It ensures your site works seamlessly for both users and search engine bots.

Think of it this way: if your website was a house, then technical SEO would be the foundation, wiring, and plumbing. Without a solid structure, even the best interior design (your content and links) won't matter.

Some of the most common aspects of technical SEO include:

  • Improving site speed and performance
  • Ensuring mobile-friendliness
  • Fixing broken links and redirects
  • Optimizing XML sitemaps and robots.txt
  • Using secure HTTPS protocol
  • Implementing structured data (schema)
  • Avoiding duplicate content with canonicals

Why is Technical SEO Important?

From my personal experience, technical SEO is the basic requirement for any SEO strategy. Without it, your site cannot perform well in search engines, regardless of how much effort you put into on-page optimization or link building.

Here's why:

  • If Google can't crawl your site properly, your content won't even show up in search results.
  • If your website is slow, visitors leave before engaging, which hurts rankings.
  • If your site isn't mobile-friendly, you're instantly at a disadvantage, since mobile-first indexing is now the standard.
  • If you have duplicate content or broken pages, your authority and trust signals weaken.
